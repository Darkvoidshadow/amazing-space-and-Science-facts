Fact 1

Did you know that the largest known structure in the Universe is a group of superclusters of galaxies called the "Hercules-Corona Borealis Great Wall"? It spans a distance of about 10 billion light-years and contains hundreds of thousands of galaxies. To put that in perspective, the Milky Way galaxy, which contains our solar system, is only about 100,000 light-years across. The sheer size of the Hercules-Corona Borealis Great Wall is mind-boggling and shows just how vast and mysterious the Universe really is!

Fact 2

Did you know that the tongue is not actually divided into regions that detect different tastes? The idea that different areas of the tongue are responsible for detecting sweet, sour, salty, and bitter tastes is a common misconception. In fact, taste buds are distributed throughout the tongue and can detect all four tastes. However, certain areas of the tongue may be more sensitive to certain tastes, which may have contributed to the misconception.

Fact 3

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy, which means it has a central bar-shaped structure surrounded by spiral arms. It is estimated to be about 100,000 light-years in diameter and contains an estimated 100 billion stars. However, not all parts of the Milky Way are equally dense. The central region of the Milky Way, which is about 10,000 light-years across, is much denser than the outer regions. In fact, the density of stars in the central region is so high that the night sky would be hundreds of times brighter than it is in our own neighborhood.

Fact 4

Did you know that lightning is a discharge of static electricity that can reach temperatures hotter than the surface of the sun? When a lightning bolt travels through the air, it heats up the air around it to an incredible temperature of up to 30,000 Kelvin (53,540 degrees Fahrenheit or 30,000 degrees Celsius). This is more than five times hotter than the surface of the sun, which has a temperature of around 5,500 Kelvin (9,932 degrees Fahrenheit or 5,500 degrees Celsius). The intense heat of a lightning bolt is what causes the air around it to rapidly expand and produce the sound of thunder.
